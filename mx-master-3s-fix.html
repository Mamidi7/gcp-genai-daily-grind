MX Master 3S

Left Click Fix Guide

Reddit + community-tested fixes. Start from top, go down.

Symptom: Left click doesn't register — need to press hard or multiple times. Right click works fine. Drag operations break mid-way.

DO NOT use WD-40 or any spray lubricant. The MX Master 3S uses an optical switch (light beam, not mechanical). WD-40 leaves a residue that blocks the light sensor and attracts more dust. Use only compressed air (dry) or 91%+ isopropyl alcohol (evaporates clean).

Fixes (Least to Most Involved)

Fix 1: Compressed Air Blast

Easy — 5 min
  • Get canned compressed air (any electronics shop, ~Rs 200)
  • Angle nozzle into the slim gap between left button and mouse body
  • Blow from multiple angles — front, side, top gap
  • Click the button 20-30 times after blowing
  • Also try: lift the left button pad slightly (plastic has flex) and blow underneath

Why this works: MX Master 3S uses an optical switch — a plastic plunger blocks/unblocks a light beam. A speck of dust in the path = missed clicks. Air dislodges it.

Fix 2: Isopropyl Alcohol + Pin Clean

Easy — 10 min
  • Lift the left button pad slightly — you'll see a plastic protrusion with a circle tip
  • Dip a pin in 91%+ isopropyl alcohol
  • Gently poke into the gap where that plastic plunger goes down
  • Follow with compressed air blast
  • Let dry 5 minutes before use

Fix 3: Reseat Ribbon Cable (Open Mouse)

Medium — 30 min

Needs: T5 Torx, PH00 Phillips, thin plastic pry tool

  • Remove the two black strips under the mouse (glide pads)
  • Unscrew all screws underneath
  • Separate top and bottom shell carefully
  • Find the ribbon cable connecting left-click PCB to main board
  • Disconnect it gently, clean contacts with alcohol, reconnect firmly
  • Also check: the thin black wire should NOT be pinched between shell halves
  • Reassemble and test

Note: Many Reddit users report the ribbon cable gets slightly loose over time. Reseating it alone fixes the issue permanently for some.

Fix 4: Reflow Solder Joints

Hard — needs soldering iron
  • Open mouse (same as Fix 3)
  • Locate the left click switch PCB (small separate board)
  • Apply flux to existing solder joints
  • Heat each joint with soldering iron until solder reflows (2-3 sec each)
  • Let cool, reassemble

Fix 5: Replace the Switch

Hard — needs desoldering

Permanent fix. The switch is a known weak point.

  • Desolder old switch from the PCB
  • Solder new switch — choose one:
EVQ-P0E07K — Clicky feel EVQ-P0D07K — Quiet like original ~Rs 50 each on Mouser/DigiKey

Quick Reference

  • Compressed air — try first, 70% success rate
  • Alcohol + pin clean — if air fails
  • Reseat ribbon cable — medium effort, high success
  • Reflow solder — if you have a soldering iron
  • Replace switch — permanent fix, ~Rs 50 part
