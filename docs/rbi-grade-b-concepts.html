Monetary Policy

RBI's most important function. High-weight topic in GA + Finance.
What is it?

Monetary Policy = RBI's control over money supply & interest rates

Monetary Policy is how RBI manages the economy by adjusting interest rates and money supply. Objective: Price stability (inflation control) + Growth. Since 2016, India uses Flexible Inflation Targeting (FIT) where inflation target is the primary goal.

Inflation — CPI vs WPI

RBI's primary mandate. Core of Flexible Inflation Targeting.
Core Concept

Inflation = rate at which prices rise over time

RBI targets CPI (Consumer Price Index) inflation at 4% with ±2% tolerance band. WPI (Wholesale Price Index) measures factory-gate prices. The key difference: CPI includes services, WPI does not. RBI's mandate is CPI inflation control.
Figure: FIT Framework — Target, Band, Failure Clause

Types of Inflation

1
Demand-Pull

Too much money chasing too few goods. Economy overheating. Solved by hiking rates.

2
Cost-Push

Input costs rise (oil, raw materials). Supply-side. Harder for RBI to control via rates alone.

3
Built-in

Workers demand higher wages → higher costs → higher prices → cycle repeats.

4
Imported

Global commodity prices, forex depreciation. E.g., crude oil price spike → domestic inflation.

RBI Functions

Central banking functions — statutory basis, direct GA questions.
RBI Act, 1934

RBI = Central Bank of India

Established: April 1, 1935 (Act passed 1934). Nationalized: Jan 1, 1949. Head office: Mumbai (moved from Kolkata in 1937). Governed by Central Board of Directors — 21 members (1 Governor + 4 Dy Governors + 4 Directors nominated by Govt + 4 nominated from local boards + others).
Figure: RBI Functions — 6 key roles + supporting institutions

35

Banking Regulation

NPAs, Basel Norms, IBC — a heavy Finance section topic.
Key Context

Bank Health = NPA management + Capital Adequacy

The RBI supervises banks through inspections (ONSITE + OFFSITE surveillance). Key metrics: NPA Ratio (bad loans / total loans), CRAR (capital / risk-weighted assets), and Basel III compliance. The IBC (Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code, 2016) is the legal framework for resolving bad loans.
Figure: NPA Framework — Classification → Regulation → Resolution → Basel

Must-Know Banking Terms

NPA — Non-Performing Asset
If interest or principal is overdue for >90 days. Sub-classified: Substandard (≤12 mo), Doubtful (>12 mo), Loss.
CRAR — Capital to Risk-Weighted Assets Ratio
Bank's capital as % of risk-weighted assets. Current minimum: 11.5% (including CCB). Also called CAR.
IBC — Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code, 2016
Time-bound resolution (330 days max). NCLT for companies, DRT for individuals. Priority: secured creditors → employees → unsecured → equity.
Basel Committee
Based in Basel, Switzerland (BIS). Basel I (1988) → Basel II (2004) → Basel III (2010, implemented in India from 2013). Focus: Capital adequacy, stress testing, liquidity ratios.

Fiscal Policy & Budget

Government's budget management. Budget 2026-27 (FY27) key numbers — exam critical.
Distinction

Fiscal Policy = Government's spending & taxation

Fiscal Policy = Government (Ministry of Finance). Monetary Policy = RBI. Fiscal policy uses the Budget (taxation + expenditure) to manage the economy. Budget 2026-27 (presented Feb 2026) will be the most heavily tested GA topic in Phase 1.

Quick revision — key formulas for Phase 1 GA + Finance.
1
GDP = C + I + G + (X-M)

Consumption + Investment + Govt Spending + Net Exports

2
GDP Deflator = (Nominal GDP / Real GDP) × 100

Measures price level change in GDP

3
Inflation = (CPIₜ − CPIₜ₋₁) / CPIₜ₋₁ × 100

Period-over-period price change

4
Fiscal Deficit = Total Exp − Total Receipts (ex borrowings)

Key budget health indicator

5
Revenue Deficit = Rev Exp − Rev Receipts

Shortfall in current income vs expenses

6
NPA Ratio = Gross NPAs / Gross Advances × 100

Bank asset quality metric

7
CRAR = Capital / Risk-Weighted Assets × 100

Basel III minimum: 11.5%

8
MSF = Repo Rate + 0.25%

Emergency borrowing rate

9
Money Multiplier = M₃ / M₀

Ratio of Broad Money to Reserve Money. RBI's actual measure (not 1/CRR).

10
Fisher Equation: Real Rate ≈ Nominal Rate − Inflation

Real interest rate approximation
