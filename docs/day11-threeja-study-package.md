## SECTION 1: GA MORNING CLASS — Government Schemes Part 2

### 1. Ayushman Bharat / Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

**Launch:** September 2018 by PM Modi
**Ministry:** Ministry of Health & Family Welfare
**Core idea:** Health insurance = Y la untadi — bazaar lo vellinappudu wallet lo dabbu lekapoina, shopkeeper manalni bayatiki pamputadu. PM-JAY alantidi — hospital ki vellinappudu treatment kosam dabbu levu ante, government eh pay chestadi.

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| Coverage | Rs 5 lakh per family per year |
| Beneficiaries | 10.74 crore families (~50 crore people) — bottom 40% of population |
| Based on | SECC 2011 data (Socio-Economic Caste Census) |
| Hospital type | Empanelled public + private hospitals |
| Cashless? | Yes — no upfront payment |
| Packages | 1,500+ medical procedures covered |

**Key facts for exam:**
- World's largest government-funded health insurance scheme
- AB-PMJAY + Health & Wellness Centres (1.5 lakh centres) = Ayushman Bharat 2 pillars
- National Health Authority (NHA) implements it
- Digitally linked — Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) 2021

**Recent updates (2025-26):**
- ABDM: Ayushman cards now have QR code + digital health records
- Telemedicine expansion through eSanjeevani platform

### 2. PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan)

**Launch:** 2019 (fully operational by 2022)
**Ministry:** Ministry of New & Renewable Energy (MNRE)
**Core idea:** Raitu ki current bill ekkuva ostundi pump set ki. Yendha balam tho free energy ivvochu? PM-KUSUM = solar pump ichhi current bill tagginchi, extra power grid ki ammey chance istundi.

| Component | What it does |
|---|---|
| Component A | 10,000 MW solar plants on barren agricultural land |
| Component B | 20 lakh standalone solar agricultural pumps |
| Component C | 15 lakh grid-connected solar agricultural pumps |

**Key facts:**
- Total outlay: Rs 34,422 crore
- Central subsidy: 30% + State subsidy: 30% + Farmer: 40%
- Farmer can sell surplus power to DISCOMs
- Targets: 30.8 GW total solar capacity from agriculture sector

**Exam angle:** Renewable + agriculture intersection. Frequently asked in GA — "Which scheme promotes solar pumps for farmers?"

---

### 3. AMRUT (Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation)

**Launch:** June 2015 (replaced JnNURM)
**Ministry:** Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs
**Core idea:** City lo water tap ivvali, sewerage system kattali, parks pettali, bus stops bagu cheyali — AMRUT is the fund + plan.

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| Focus cities | 500 cities (all cities > 1 lakh population) |
| Duration | 2015-2020 (extended to 2024) |
| Outlay | Rs 50,000 crore |
| 5 thrust areas | Water supply, Sewerage, Storm water drains, Urban transport, Green spaces |

**Key facts:**
- Mission 2.0 launched 2021 — extended to all urban areas
- Focus on universal water supply coverage
- PPP mode encouraged
- City-level 5-year action plans mandatory

**AMRUT 2.0 (2021-2024):**
- Added: Digital water management, rejuvenation of water bodies
- Target: 100% household water tap connections

---

### 4. Smart Cities Mission

**Launch:** June 2015
**Ministry:** Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs
**Core idea:** City ni smartphone la manage cheyali — sensors, data, digital connectivity. Traffic, water, lighting — everything connected and optimized.

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| Selected cities | 100 cities (originally 98, added 2 later) |
| Duration | 5 years per city (rolling) |
| Outlay | Rs 48,000 crore (Central) + matching State/ULB |
| SPV | Special Purpose Vehicle — company-like structure for execution |

**Core infrastructure elements:**
- Adequate water supply
- Assured electricity
- Sanitation (including solid waste management)
- Efficient urban mobility
- Affordable housing
- Robust IT connectivity
- Governance (e-governance, citizen engagement)
- Safety (especially for women, children, elderly)

**Exam facts:**
- First 20 cities announced Jan 2016
- Area-based development + pan-city solutions
- Lighthouse cities: Surat, Pune, Ahmedabad, Bhopal, etc.
- PM says: "Cities should be engines of growth"

### 5. Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) / Housing for All

**Launch:** June 2015 (replaces Indira Awas Yojana)
**Ministry:** Ministry of Housing & Urban Affairs (Urban) / Ministry of Rural Development (Rural — PMAY-G)
**Core idea:** Prathi kutumbaniki oka illu. EWS/LIG families ki subsidized loans + subsidy amount direct bank account ki.

| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| Target | 2.95 crore houses (Urban) + 2.95 crore (Rural) = ~6 crore total |
| By year | 2022 (extended to 2024) |
| Beneficiary | EWS (annual income < Rs 3 lakh), LIG (< Rs 6 lakh), MIG (< Rs 18 lakh) |
| Subsidy | Rs 1 lakh to Rs 2.67 lakh depending on income category |
| Interest subsidy | 3% to 6.5% on home loans under CLSS |

**PMAY Components:**
1. **In-Situ Slum Redevelopment** — Slum dwellers get pucca houses on same land
2. **Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS)** — Interest subsidy on home loans
3. **Affordable Housing in Partnership** — Public-private partnerships
4. **Beneficiary-led Individual House Construction** — Rs 1.5 lakh assistance for own construction

**CLSS interest subsidy table (memorize this):**

| Income | Loan max | Subsidy % | Max subsidy | Tenure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| EWS (<3L) | Rs 6 lakh | 6.50% | Rs 2.67 lakh | 20 yrs |
| LIG (3-6L) | Rs 6 lakh | 6.50% | Rs 2.67 lakh | 20 yrs |
| MIG I (6-12L) | Rs 9 lakh | 4.00% | Rs 2.35 lakh | 20 yrs |
| MIG II (12-18L) | Rs 12 lakh | 3.00% | Rs 2.30 lakh | 20 yrs |

**Key exam facts:**
- PMAY-Urban + PMAY-Gramin = total "Housing for All by 2022" mission
- Houses have mandatory provisions: toilet, LPG connection, electricity, water
- Geo-tagging of all houses mandatory
- Central & State governments share cost 60:40 (plain areas), 90:10 (NE, hilly, Himalayan states)
