01

The Only Formula You Need

Everything in Probability is just this one formula applied again and again.

P(Event) = Favorable Outcomes / Total Possible Outcomes
Telugu Analogy

Probability = Bag eesi theesukunna la

Nuvvu oka bag lo 10 beads vesaru — 4 red, 6 blue. Nuvvu eyes close chesi oka bead teestav. Red bead vasthada? Favorable = 4 (red beads count). Total = 10 (total beads). Probability = 4/10 = 0.4 = 40%.

That's ALL probability is: Count what you want ÷ Count everything possible. Exam lo dice, cards, coins anni idi step 1: count favorable, step 2: divide by total.

Important: Range of Probability

Probability always between 0 and 1 (or 0% to 100%).

P = 0 → impossible ("pig will fly tomorrow")
P = 1 → certain ("sun will rise tomorrow")
P = 0.5 → even chance (coin toss heads)

The easiest topic. Once you understand this, dice and cards follow the same pattern.

Oka coin ki 2 sides = Head, Tail. Total possible outcomes = 2. That's it. All coin questions are just "how many heads count ÷ 2".

Playing Cards

Many students panic at cards. Don't. It's just a counting game. Memorize the 52-card structure once and you're done.

Telugu Analogy

Cards = 52 beads in a bag — but sorted by 4 groups, 13 each

Nuvvu oka bag lo 52 beads vesaru. 4 colors — Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs, Spades. Each color lo 13 beads — Ace, 2 to 10, Jack, Queen, King. Alage. Drawing a card means taking one bead from this bag. Count what you want. Divide by 52.

05

Balls & Urns

Same formula. Count what you want ÷ Count everything.

1
Without Replacement

Ball drawn and NOT put back. Total decreases by 1 every draw. Second draw has fewer options.

2
With Replacement

Ball drawn then PUT BACK. Total stays same every draw. Each draw is independent (like fresh start).

Step-by-Step — Balls Problem

Bag: 5 Red + 3 Blue = 8 Total

Q: Draw one ball. P(Red) = ?
A: Red balls = 5. Total = 8. P(Red) = 5/8.

Q: Draw one ball. P(Not Red) = ?
A: Two approaches: (1) P(Blue) = 3/8. (2) 1 - P(Red) = 1 - 5/8 = 3/8. Same answer.

Q: Draw two balls WITHOUT replacement. P(Both Red) = ?
A: Step 1: P(1st Red) = 5/8. Step 2: P(2nd Red | 1st was Red) = 4/7 (one red removed). Answer = 5/8 x 4/7 = 20/56 = 5/14.

06

Addition Rule — P(A or B)

P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)
Applied to Dice

P(Even number or Number > 4) on single dice

P(Even): 2,4,6 = 3/6 = 1/2

P(>4): 5,6 = 2/6 = 1/3

P(Even AND >4): 6 (the number common in both) = 1/6

P(Even OR >4): 1/2 + 1/3 - 1/6 = 3/6 + 2/6 - 1/6 = 4/6 = 2/3

Quick check: Numbers 2,4,5,6 = 4 out of 6 = 2/3. Correct!

07

Complement Rule

P(Not A) = 1 - P(A)

If something is hard to count directly, count its opposite and subtract from 1.

Example — "At least one" trick

Two dice. P(At least one 6) = ?

Direct approach: Count all pairs where at least one dice shows 6. Many cases.

Complement approach (easier): P(No 6) = P(both are NOT 6) = 5/6 x 5/6 = 25/36
P(At least one 6) = 1 - 25/36 = 11/36

Verify: Total = 36. Pairs with at least one 6: (6,1)(6,2)(6,3)(6,4)(6,5)(6,6)(1,6)(2,6)(3,6)(4,6)(5,6) = 11. 11/36 ✓

When to use complement: Whenever you see "at least one" or "none" in the question. It's almost always easier to find P(none) first, then subtract from 1.

Final Word

Your Neighbourhood Tutor

Probability is just counting.

Three simple steps for ANY probability question:

Step 1: Understand what the Event is (what do you want to happen?)
Step 2: Count Favorable outcomes (how many ways that event can happen?)
Step 3: Count Total outcomes (what's everything that could possibly happen?)
Step 4: Favorable ÷ Total. Done.

Dice = 6 faces. Cards = 52 deck, 4 suits of 13 each. Coins = 2 sides. Two dice = 36 pairs. This is ALL the memorization you need. Everything else is just applying these numbers.
