Financial Markets

Money Market vs Capital Market — the two halves of India's financial system. ~5-8 Qs in GA.
Telugu Analogy

Money Market = short-term loan la; Capital Market = long-term investment la

Nuvvu friend ki Rs.1000 ichav, next week return chestadu — adi Money Market la. Short term, < 1 year. Banks, RBI, NBFCs ila institutions madhya jarige lending/borrowing. Kaani nuvvu oka company stock konnav — 5 years hold chestav, price perigaka ammestav — adi Capital Market la. Long term, companies ki capital raise cheyyadaniki.
Figure: Two-market structure — Money Market (short, institutional) vs Capital Market (long, public)

Money Market — Deep Dive (GA High Weight)

1
CBLO — Collateralized Borrowing & Lending Obligation

CCIL runs it. Fully collateralized (govt securities backing). 1-365 day maturity. Replaced the old "call money" unsecured system. RBI exam fav.

2
CP — Commercial Paper

Unsecured promissory note. Issued by large corporates to raise short-term funds. Min: 7 days, Max: 1 year. Rated by CRISIL/ICRA/CARE. NBFCs also now allowed to issue CP.

3
CD — Certificate of Deposit

Bank/FI issued negotiable instrument. Min: 7 days, Max: 1 year. Like a bank's own promissory note. Tradable in secondary market.

4
T-Bills — Treasury Bills

Govt issued, zero coupon (sold at discount). 91-day, 182-day, 364-day. Safest money market instrument. No default risk — sovereign backing.

Capital Market — Key Bodies

S
SEBI

Est. 1988 (statutory 1992). Head: Madhabi Puri Buch. Regulates stock exchanges, mutual funds, FIIs, merchant bankers. Not RBI — this is a common distractor trap.

N
NSE & BSE

NSE = 1992 (India's largest by volume). BSE = 1875 (Asia's oldest). Both under SEBI. NIFTY 50 (NSE), SENSEX 30 (BSE).

G
G-Sec Market

Government Securities — RBI manages issuance. G-SAP (Securities Acquisition Program) = RBI buys G-Secs from market to inject liquidity.

Exam Tip: Question often goes: "Which regulator oversees the Capital Market?" — SEBI. "Money Market?" — RBI. Don't mix them up. CPA auditor mind: think jurisdiction boundaries.

NPA & Banking Health

You already know NPAs from audit. Now learn them from RBI regulator's angle. ~10 Qs guaranteed across GA + Finance.
Telugu Analogy

NPA = Friend ki loan ichav, 90 days interest kooda ivvaledu

Bank oka customer ki loan ichindi. Customer EMI kattaledu. 90 days ayyaka — bank books lo adi NPA ayipoyindi. Nuvvu CA ga provisioning, write-offs ani chesav kada? Same concept, but RBI sets the rules. Ippudu bank "eh interest vastunnattu" chupinchakudadu — kattaleka pote NPA gurthinchali.

NPA Classification — The 90-Day Rule

A
Standard Asset

Regular payments. Provisioning: 0.4% (agri/SME: 0.25%). Normal loan.

B
Sub-Standard

NPA < 12 months. Provisioning: 15% (secured), 25% (unsecured).

C
Doubtful

NPA > 12 months. Higher provisioning. D1/D2/D3 tiers based on time + collateral.

D
Loss Asset

Uncollectible. 100% provisioning. Written off (but not forgiven — still recoverable legally).

The 3 Weapons: IBC + SARFAESI + ARC

ToolYearWho Uses ItPurpose IBC (Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code) 2016 Creditor / Corporate Debtor Time-bound insolvency resolution (180 days + 90 ext). NCLT approves resolution plan. Biggest impact on NPA recovery. SARFAESI Act 2002 Banks / NBFCs / ARCs Seize and sell secured assets WITHOUT court. 60 days notice. No DRT/Court needed. Used for secured NPAs. ARC (Asset Reconstruction Company) 2003 (SARFAESI enabled) Banks → sell NPAs to ARCs Buy bad loans from banks at discount, then recover. Example: ARCIL (India's first), NARCL (Bad Bank). Clean up bank balance sheets.
Key Formula — NPA Ratios (Exam Numericals)

Gross NPA Ratio = Gross NPAs / Gross Advances x 100

Net NPA Ratio = (Gross NPAs - Provisions) / Net Advances x 100

Net NPA ALWAYS lower than Gross NPA. Difference = provisioning quality. Smaller gap = better provisioned bank.

Provisioning Coverage Ratio (PCR)

PCR = Provisions held / Gross NPAs x 100

RBI wants PCR > 70%. Higher = safer bank. PSBs traditionally had low PCR (50-60%). Post-IBC, improving.

CA Advantage: You've seen NPA entries — debits to Provisions, credits to Loans. But the exam asks policy questions: "What should RBI do if PCR drops?" (Answer: restrict dividends, tighten lending limits). Think from regulator's desk, not auditor's working papers.

External Sector

Balance of Payments, Forex, Capital Account — high weight in ESI Paper 1 + GA. ~8-12 Qs.
Telugu Analogy

BoP = Personal Budget la — Income (exports) vs Expense (imports) ka ledger

Nee personal ledger lo income side salary vuntadi, expense side rent, food vuntadi. Alane — India's Balance of Payments (BoP) ledger: Credit side lo exports, remittances, FDI. Debit side lo imports, foreign travel, interest payments. Current Account = day-to-day transactions; Capital Account = investment-type transactions.
Figure: Current Account + Capital Account = BoP. CAD vs Capital Flows — the twin deficit story.

Forex Reserves — India's 4 Components

F
Foreign Currency Assets (FCA)

Biggest chunk (~90%). Held in USD, EUR, GBP, JPY. Also includes SDR holdings. Interest earned = part of reserves accretion.

G
Gold Reserves

~800 tonnes. Held at RBI + Bank of England + BIS. Revalued quarterly based on international gold prices.

S
SDR (Special Drawing Rights)

IMF's reserve asset. Like a "credit line" countries can draw against. India's SDR quota = ~2.75% of global. Not actual currency — a claim on freely usable currencies.

R
Reserve Tranche Position (RTP)

India's deposits with IMF. The 25% of quota that countries pay in reserve assets. Can borrow this instantly — no conditions.

Capital Account Convertibility (CAC)

EXAM DEFINITION — What is CAC?

Freedom to convert local financial assets into foreign financial assets and vice versa at market-determined exchange rates. India has partial/limited CAC — FDI allowed freely, but Indian residents can't freely invest abroad beyond LRS ($250K/year limit). Full CAC was recommended by Tarapore Committee (1997, 2006) but not yet implemented fully.

Exam Trap: "India has full Capital Account Convertibility" — FALSE. Only Current Account is fully convertible (since 1994 under Article VIII of IMF). Capital Account is partially convertible. This is a top-3 distractor in RBI exams.

Reasoning — Syllogism

Phase 1: 15-20 marks reasoning. Syllogism is the most formulaic — master it, and you get 5 guaranteed marks in 2 minutes.
Telugu Analogy

Syllogism = Venn diagram game — Statements → Diagram → Check Conclusion

Oka example: "All fruits are sweet. Mango is a fruit. Conclusion: Mango is sweet." Logic correct — diagram lo fruits circle lo mango vundi, sweet tag vundi. Idedo obvious ga vundi kada? But exam lo "All A are B. Some B are C. Conclusion: Some A are C" — idi wrong. Diagram draw chestene telustadi A and C overlap ledu ani.

The 4 Statement Types (Only-Only — No Variations)

TypeStatementVenn DiagramExample Universal PositiveAll A are BA circle completely inside BAll banks are regulated Universal NegativeNo A are BA and B circles separateNo bank is unregulated Particular PositiveSome A are BA and B overlapping partlySome loans are NPAs Particular NegativeSome A are NOT BPart of A outside BSome customers are not defaulters

The Golden Rules (Memorize These)

1
Negative Rule

If one premise is negative, the conclusion MUST be negative. Two negatives → no conclusion.

2
Middle Term Rule

Middle term (the term common to both premises) must be distributed at least once. Otherwise → no conclusion.

3
Particular Rule

If one premise is particular ("Some"), the conclusion MUST be particular. Two particular premises → no conclusion.

4
Complementary Pair

"Some A are B" + "Some A are NOT B" = one of them ALWAYS true (either-or case). Both particular = not automatically false.

Solved Example — 3-Point Method

Statements: All Banks are Companies. Some Companies are PSBs.

Step 1: Draw "Banks" circle inside "Companies" circle.
Step 2: Draw "PSBs" circle partly overlapping "Companies" — but not necessarily entering "Banks".
Step 3: Check each conclusion against the diagram.

Wrong Conclusion: "Some Banks are PSBs" (diagram shows PSB could be outside Banks)
Wrong Conclusion: "All PSBs are Banks" (PSBs could be partly outside)
Correct (if asked): "Some Companies are Banks" (true — Banks entirely inside Companies)

RBI Exam Reality: Direct Syllogism is 3-4 questions. Plus "Reverse Syllogism" (given conclusions, pick matching statements) is another 3-4 questions. Total: ~7-8 reasoning marks from this one topic. Master it by drawing diagrams visually — never memorize rules alone.

ESI Preview — GDP & National Income

Phase 2's Paper 1 (Economic & Social Issues — 100 marks). Start building the macro framework now. GDP calculation is the foundation.
Telugu Analogy

GDP = India's total income — like family's combined annual salary

Oka family lo father business chestunnadu, mother job chestundi, son freelancing — combined income anedi family living standard ki indicator. Alane GDP: country lo one year lo produce chesina total goods & services value. India produced goods and services worth ~$3.9 trillion in 2025-26.
Figure: GDP can be measured from 3 angles — all converge to same value. India primarily uses Production (GVA) method.

India's GDP Calculation — GVA Approach

KEY — What India Actually Uses

India (CSO/NSO) calculates GDP primarily by GVA (Gross Value Added) method — the Production method. For each sector, GVA = value of output minus value of intermediate consumption. Then GDP = sum of GVA + taxes - subsidies. Quarterly GDP estimates come from corporate filings, IIP data, and sector surveys.

Must-Know Concepts

R
Real vs Nominal GDP

Nominal = current price. Real = constant price (base year 2011-12). Real GDP removes inflation effect. "GDP Growth Rate" always means Real GDP Growth.

D
GDP Deflator

GDP Deflator = Nominal GDP / Real GDP x 100. It measures overall economy inflation — broader than CPI or WPI because it covers all goods/services produced domestically.

B
Base Year Revision

Current base: 2011-12. RBI has recommended shifting to 2020-21 base. Every revision changes GDP numbers (shows truer picture). Always check what's the latest.

G
GNI vs GDP

GNI = GDP + net income from abroad (remittances, investment income). For India, GNI > GDP (we receive ~$125B in remittances — largest in world).

GDP = GVA (at basic prices) + Product Taxes - Product Subsidies

India's FY26 GDP growth estimated at 6.5-7.0% (RBI projection). FY25 actual: 6.6%.

Phase 2 ESI Strategy: GDP chapter is foundational — Monetary Policy, Fiscal Policy, Employment, and Inflation ALL connect back to GDP. If you understand GDP calculation by heart, the rest of ESI becomes "GDP ke extensions" — less new material to learn.
